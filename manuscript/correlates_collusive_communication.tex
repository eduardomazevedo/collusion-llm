We now examine the correlates of collusive communication. We will consider three types of correlates: firm sector, firm size, and call year. These covariates are not available for all firms, so the sample sizes for each of these analyses differ somewhat. There are \data{correlates_collusive_communication/sector_valid_observations_int} firms in the sector analysis, \data{correlates_collusive_communication/mkvalt_valid_observations_int} firms in the size analysis, and \data{correlates_collusive_communication/year_valid_observations_int} firms in the year analysis.

We have thee main measures of collusive communication:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Whether a transcript is in the LLM flagged set.
    \item Whether a transcript is in the LLM flagged set and also validated as collusive by the followup LLM queries.
    \item Whether a transcript is in the LLM flagged set, validated as collusive by the followup LLM queries, and also confirmed as collusive by human audit.
\end{enumerate}

Unfortunately, the human audit validated sample is small, so that our estimates are very noisy. Thus, we report results for the first two measures.
